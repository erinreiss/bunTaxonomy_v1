The average Chinese bakery in Chinatown offers over 40 different kinds of buns, cakes, and other treats. But signage is often vague as to what each bun is like and what delicious ingredents are inside the fluffy white dough or fried crispy shell. This guide is meant to help you expand your repetoire in a typical Chinese bakery — each bun has its name in English and Chinese, a list of common indredients, and a pronunciation guide. We hope it will encourage you to explore new buns of your dreams!

Note: The name of a given bun and it's ingredients will vary bakery to bakery — these names and ingredient lists reflect what is served at Lucky King Bakery on Grand Street in Manhattan's Chinatown and might not perfectly match the bakery you choose to visit!
